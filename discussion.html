How do you define a good website?

A great website often comes from a good web design. Gehrke(1999) said that a successful ecommerce website depends alot on the web design that is approriate for the user. Zhou et al.(2009) have also made findings that web design does indeed has a great effect on being a good website. While there are no clear cut way to define a good web design, there are a few factors that contributes to define a good web design. The Originality, Simplicity, Consistency, User-Friendliness and Speed are core aspects of having a good web design of a website (Cited by Najjar, 2013).

Originality can be defined as differentiating your web design from the common designs.

Simplicity is the antonyms of complicated. Your Web design should look organized and less complex.

Consistency is to keep your brand aligned across all pages. It is not advisable to have too many colours, styles or themes

User-Friendliness is to keep your brand aligned across all pages. It is not advisable to have too many colours, styles or themes
